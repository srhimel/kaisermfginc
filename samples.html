Case Studies: a proven track record

The diversity of sewn products generated by the KAISER team has been immense over the past 50 years. Through design, development and construction, we have solved problems for hundreds of large and small companies from more than 20 industries.

  

The case studies outlined here are a small sampling of the scope of our work and depth of expertise.

Traveling Kiosks

The Disney Company is masterful at selling merchandise related to its franchises, in this case Disney on Ice. With four different shows and multiple touring companies for each, that's lots of Disney kiosks and paraphernalia traveling around the world to a new city every week.

The Challenge: Equipment was continually getting damaged. They needed a better way to package the kiosks and merchandise to ensure it was well protected during transport and easy to unpack and pack.

KAISER's Solution: We studied the wear and tear on the current equipment and learned more about how the marketeers were actually using it. We then designed and constructed soft, padded packaging for each of the component parts, all of which fit compactly and securely into a larger shipping container.

Pest Control

Bed bugs. No one wants them. The hospitality industry is aggressively fighting them and ________ Pest Control is helping. Heat is the safest, cheapest, and most effective way to eradicate bed bugs, which cannot live at temperatures above 115 degrees Fahrenheit.

The Challenge: The heat generated by the large heating units being placed in hotel rooms also set off the sprinkler system. In addition, the heating units consumed large amounts of electricity.

KAISER's Solution: We designed and constructed insulated covers for the sprinkler heads. Made from nylon and styrofoam, now the sprinkler system cannot detect the elevated temperatures. We also designed an inflatable divider system with baffles to isolate discrete areas for treatment, reducing heat loss and energy consumption.

Noise Reduction

Automotive assembly lines can be loud. OSHA regulations require employers supply hearing protection to all employees exposed to noises of 85 decibels or greater, averaged over an 8-hour time-weighted period.

The Challenge: Ear protection is expensive. Employee may forget to wear it. It can be uncomfortable. Enforcement becomes an issue. Is there a better solution? ________ had already identified what pieces of equipment were operating outside the sound limits.

KAISER's Solution: Box in the offending equipment. with custom-made accoustic panels, resulting in a quieter work environment and cost savings by eliminating the need for ear protection.

Athletic Facility Padding

Every angle, hard edge and upright in sports venues — from hockey rinks to indoor soccer to ski mountains — needs protective padding. KAISER works with a distributors across the country to supply custom padding for almost every sports venue imaginable. The Pettit National Ice Center in, an U.S. Olympic training site in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, is one example.

The Challenge: This showcase athletic facility needed custom padding to cushion in the 400-meter indoor speed skating oval, two Olympic-size ice rinks and an elevated run/walk track. The configurations had to be removable to accommodate long- and short-track speed skating, hockey, figure skating and curling.

KAISER's Solution: Working with a distributor, we delivered hundreds of different padding configurations made from high density foam and vinyl in red, white and blue. Take a look at the Pettit National Ice Center website to see the results.
