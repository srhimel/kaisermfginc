Design and Development

Whether you are an engineer with design specifications in hand, a marketeer with a packaging dilemma that needs solving or an entrepreneur with a hand-crafted model, KAISER is here to help.

Through every step of the design and development process, we are committed to making sure the finished product works as designed and is produced in the most cost-effective manner possible.

Ideation

First, we'll learn as much as we can about your product. How will the product be used? In what environment? Is it a component of a larger product? Must it meet a set of domestic or international standards? Are you seeking to replace an existing sewn product with a more cost-effective solution? What quantity is needed? What is your timeline?

We can begin work from any of the following:

 
  • a working model
  • a photograph
  • a simple sketch with dimensions
  • an engineering drawing
  • what you are currently using but wish to improve

Prototype

Next, we build a working prototype using traditional manual methods. Like a tailor, we take two-dimensional fabric and drape it to form your three dimensional product, determining where seams, darts and fasteners will go. We also research fabric alternatives at this point.

If you already have a working model, we will evaluate its construction and make recommendations for improvement. Once you've signed off on the prototype, it becomes the foundation for creating a pattern for your product.

Pattern Development

We create patterns using an advanced digitizing process. This enables us to translate the details of your prototype into data points that are then imported into industry-leading StyleCAD software. Using this system, your pattern will be stored and can be modified on the computer, When ready, it will be integrated with our Pathfinder cutting machine. StyleCAD features include:

Review and Production

Throughout the design and development process, we will seek your input and, as we meet milestones, get your sign-off. You will be closely involved as the design and pattern-making progress.
